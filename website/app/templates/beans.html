Arabica and Robusta Comparison

Flavor profile

Arabica beans have a more complex and multifaceted flavor, often described as sweet with notes of fruit, sugar, and berries, as well as a slight acidity. Robusta, on the other hand, is known for its strong, sharp flavor with notes of nuts, chocolate, and a more pronounced bitterness.

Caffeine content

Arabica beans contain less caffeine than robusta beans—about 1.5% versus 2.7%. This lower caffeine content contributes to the milder and less bitter taste of arabica.

Growing conditions

Arabica plants are more delicate and require special conditions to grow. Robusta, on the other hand, is more resilient and can grow at low altitudes and in hot, humid climates.

Shape and size

The main difference between Arabica and Robusta beans is their shape and groove: Arabica beans are long and oval with a curved S-shaped groove. Robusta beans are round, smaller in size, and have a straight groove.
